# Example Output — FormFocus TikTok Script Package

**Brand:** FormFocus — AI form builder for small business owners  
**Target Audience:** "Overloaded Olivia" — 28-40 year old small business owner drowning in manual admin work  
**Platform:** TikTok  
**Variations:** 3 (each with a different hook type)

---

## Variation 1

**Hook Mechanic Label:** Relatable Pain

### Script

```
[HOOK - 0:00-0:03]
Visual: Creator at a cluttered desk, phone in hand, slightly frustrated look — natural morning light
Spoken: "I was spending an hour every single day just chasing down client intake forms."
Text overlay: "1 HOUR. Every day. On forms."

[BODY - 0:03-0:22]
Visual: Quick cut to phone screen showing a client WhatsApp thread full of back-and-forth questions
Spoken: "Like — I'd send the form link, they'd text me the answers anyway, I'd manually copy everything into my spreadsheet... it was a whole thing."
Text overlay: "The spreadsheet era 😐"

Visual: Cut back to creator, leaning forward like she's sharing a secret
Spoken: "Then I found FormFocus. I built my entire client intake in like 15 minutes — it auto-fills into my CRM, sends the client a confirmation, and I never touch it again."
Text overlay: "15 min setup → never again"

Visual: Quick screen recording of a clean, branded form on phone
Spoken: "My clients think I hired an assistant. I didn't."
Text overlay: "No assistant. Just this."

[CTA - 0:22-0:28]
Visual: Back to creator, slight smirk
Spoken: "If you're still doing this manually, I'll drop the link in my bio."
Text overlay: "Link in bio — free to try"
```

**Director's Note:** Film at a real home office or coffee shop desk — lived-in, not staged. Creator energy should feel like a peer sharing a solution, not a spokesperson. Keep the phone screen recording short (2-3 seconds) and use native screen recording, not slick screen capture software. No ring light — window light preferred.

**Ready-to-Post Caption:** Spent an hour a day on client forms until I found this — never again 😮‍💨 #smallbizowner #clientonboarding #businesstips #formfocus

## Variation 2

**Hook Mechanic Label:** Story/Confession

### Script

```
[HOOK - 0:00-0:03]
Visual: Creator walking into frame from off-camera, hand slightly covering mouth
Spoken: "I'm embarrassed it took me three years to stop doing this manually."
Text overlay: "3 years. I'm not proud of it."

[BODY - 0:03-0:24]
Visual: Cut to creator sitting down, elbows on desk — direct address
Spoken: "Okay so. I run a small consulting business. Six clients. And every single time I onboarded someone new, I was literally emailing back and forth for days to collect the same information I needed from every single person."
Text overlay: "Same questions. Every. Time."

Visual: Quick zoom to a chaotic-looking email inbox (blurred for privacy)
Spoken: "Name, business details, goals, payment info — all in different threads, some in my DMs, one client literally texted me her credit card number."
Text overlay: "She texted me her card number 💀"

Visual: Cut back to creator, relieved expression
Spoken: "FormFocus changed the whole thing. One smart form, one link, everything in one place. My new client onboarding now takes me — I timed it — four minutes."
Text overlay: "4 minutes. Timed it."

[CTA - 0:24-0:30]
Visual: Creator holds up phone showing a clean form confirmation screen
Spoken: "Tell me if your onboarding is still a mess — because mine was, and I'll share exactly what I switched to."
Text overlay: "Comment 'FORMS' and I'll share it"
```

**Director's Note:** The hook lands harder if the creator physically walks into frame mid-laugh, like they just thought of this to share. Energy is warm and self-deprecating — not embarrassed, more "I can't believe I waited this long." The comment bait CTA ("Comment 'FORMS'") will drive engagement for the algorithm; make sure the creator plans to reply in comments with the link.

**Ready-to-Post Caption:** 3 years doing client onboarding the hard way before I found this 😭 comment FORMS if you want the link #onboarding #smallbusiness #formfocus #bizowner

## Variation 3

**Hook Mechanic Label:** Contrarian

### Script

```
[HOOK - 0:00-0:03]
Visual: Creator looking directly into camera, slightly raised eyebrow
Spoken: "Everyone says just use Google Forms — here's why that's actually killing your close rate."
Text overlay: "Google Forms is killing your close rate"

[BODY - 0:03-0:23]
Visual: Quick cut to a generic-looking Google Form on screen
Spoken: "Google Forms looks fine to you. To your potential client? It looks like you put in zero effort."
Text overlay: "What your client actually sees"

Visual: Cut back to creator at desk
Spoken: "When I switched to FormFocus, my forms had my branding, my tone, smart conditional questions — so clients only see what's relevant to them. It felt like a professional company."
Text overlay: "Smart forms — only asks what matters"

Visual: Close-up of a branded, clean form on phone screen — scrolling slowly
Spoken: "And the wild part — my form completion rate went from like 60% to basically 100%. People actually finish it because it doesn't feel like homework."
Text overlay: "Completion rate: 60% → nearly 100%"

Visual: Back to creator, direct and matter-of-fact
Spoken: "I'm not saying Google Forms is bad. I'm saying it's not built for making a first impression."
Text overlay: "Not bad. Just not built for this."

[CTA - 0:23-0:30]
Visual: Creator points at camera
Spoken: "If you care about how clients experience you from day one, I'll drop the link in my bio."
Text overlay: "Link in bio — see the difference"
```

**Director's Note:** The contrarian hook requires confident, calm delivery — not aggressive. Creator should not seem like they're dunking on Google, just sharing a considered opinion. Film with the creator at a desk or standing in a clean background. The form screen recording should be slow and deliberate — let the branding sit for 2 full seconds so viewers can register it. Avoid jump cuts in this variation; the pacing should feel measured and credible.

**Ready-to-Post Caption:** Google Forms is fine — but it's not making the impression you think it is 👀 #smallbizowner #clientexperience #formfocus #businesstools #ugcad
