# Trend Frameworks for UGC Research

## Identifying Fresh vs. Oversaturated Hook Formats

Three signals determine whether a hook format is fresh or burned out:

**Volume:** Count how many brands in a category are running the same hook structure within a 30-day window. When mid-tier and small brands start copying a format that originally worked for a major brand, saturation is accelerating. A hook used by 2-3 brands is fresh. Used by 10+ brands in the same category? It's peaked.

**Brand diversity:** A format being used across multiple unrelated categories (beauty, SaaS, food, fitness) signals it has crossed from niche-effective to generic. If the same hook structure appears in both a skincare ad and a B2B software ad in the same week, it's no longer a differentiator — it's wallpaper.

**Engagement rate trends:** Fresh formats generate high comment volume and saves. Watch for comments that reference the format itself ("omg this format is so good") vs. comments that mock it ("another one of these ads"). When users start commenting on the formula rather than the content, the format has lost its transparency — viewers are pattern-matching to "ad" before the hook lands.

## The Trend Lifecycle

Every UGC format follows a predictable arc:

**Discovery:** A creator or brand accidentally stumbles onto a format that performs unexpectedly well. Engagement spikes, but almost nobody else is doing it yet. This is the best time to use a format — audiences haven't built resistance to it.

**Growth:** Other creators and brands notice the performance and begin adopting the format. Engagement is still strong because the format feels relatively fresh. This is still a good window — early enough to benefit, broad enough that the format is proven.

**Peak:** The format is widely recognized as "what's working." Trade publications, marketing podcasts, and brand teams start citing it. Adoption accelerates. Engagement rates begin declining as audience familiarity increases.

**Saturation:** The format appears in feeds so frequently that viewers auto-identify it as an ad pattern before the hook completes. Scroll-past rates increase. Brands that were early adopters have already moved on. Late adopters are now running a format the audience has trained itself to ignore.

**Dead:** The format is actively mocked or generates negative sentiment. Running it signals creative laziness and damages brand perception.

*UGC example:* The "I tried X for 30 days" format went from Discovery (rare, high engagement) to Dead (parody accounts mocking it) in roughly 18 months on TikTok.

## Using the Oversaturation Warning List to Differentiate

The Oversaturation Warning List isn't just a list of things to avoid — it's a map of creative opportunity. When a format saturates, the opposite approach often outperforms it because it pattern-interrupts against what the audience expects.

If "day-in-my-life routine" content is oversaturated, "anti-routine" or "I stopped doing the thing everyone recommends" tends to outperform. If social proof stacks ("5 reasons this changed my life") are burned out, a single brutal honest take with no list structure often cuts through.

Use the Warning List to brief creators with a "not this, but the opposite of this" direction. The contrast drives completion rates precisely because it violates expectations built up by the saturated format.

## Platform-Specific Trend Velocity

**TikTok:** Fastest trend velocity of any platform. A format can go from Discovery to Saturation in 4-8 weeks during peak virality. Trend half-life is short — research done more than 6 weeks ago may already be outdated. Prioritize recency when sourcing TikTok trend data.

**Instagram Reels:** Slightly slower than TikTok, with a 6-12 week typical lifecycle. Reels often adopts TikTok trends with a 2-4 week lag, which creates a brief arbitrage window where a TikTok-proven format is still fresh on Instagram.

**YouTube Shorts:** Slower velocity, 8-16 week cycles. Shorts rewards slightly more produced content and hooks with clearer informational value. Trends from TikTok arrive here last and stay viable longest.

**LinkedIn:** Slowest trend velocity. Formats can remain effective for 6-12 months before saturation. LinkedIn audiences are smaller and less format-sensitive — the emphasis is on authority and specificity of insight rather than hook novelty. A "hot take" format that burned out on TikTok in 2023 may still perform on LinkedIn in 2024.

## The Opposite Trend Technique

When researching a category where most formats are at Peak or Saturation, apply this technique systematically:

1. List the dominant format conventions (fast cuts, upbeat music, transformation reveals, list structures, creator smiling at camera).
2. Invert each convention (slow pacing, silence or ambient audio, no reveal, single continuous thought, creator not looking at camera).
3. Evaluate which inversions feel genuinely interesting vs. gimmicky.

The inversions that feel interesting — not just different — are candidate formats to test. The goal isn't to be contrarian for its own sake; it's to find the white space that audiences have been primed to find refreshing precisely because they've been oversaturated with the dominant pattern.
