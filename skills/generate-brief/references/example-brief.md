# Creative Brief: FormFocus — "Forms That Actually Work" Campaign

**Brief Version:** 1.0
**Prepared for:** FormFocus UGC Campaign — Q2 Launch
**Platforms:** TikTok, Instagram Reels, YouTube Shorts
**Content Type:** UGC-style short-form video ads
**Creator Count:** 8–12 creators across verticals

---

## Campaign Overview

FormFocus is an AI-powered form builder built for small business owners who are done fighting with clunky tools and half-baked integrations. It auto-generates branded forms, handles payment collection, and pushes everything straight into their CRM — no code, no consultant, no second tab.

This campaign launches the product to solo operators and micro-business owners who currently duct-tape together Typeform, Stripe, and a spreadsheet — and hate every minute of it. The goal is direct response: drive free trial signups. The creative angle is frustration-to-relief. We're not selling features. We're selling the feeling of having it handled.

**Campaign goal:** 2,000 free trial signups in 30 days
**Primary KPI:** Free trial start (tracked via UTM + in-app event)
**Secondary KPI:** Cost per trial start under $12

---

## Target Audience Profile

**Primary Persona: "Overloaded Olivia"**

- **Who she is:** 31–42 years old, runs a service-based small business (photography studio, coaching practice, home services, boutique agency). Solo or 1–3 person team. Wears every hat.
- **Where she is:** On her phone between client calls. Following business productivity accounts on TikTok and Instagram. Googles things like "easy form builder with payment" at 10pm.
- **Revenue range:** $80K–$250K/year. Profitable but always stretched thin on time.
- **Tech comfort:** Competent but not technical. Uses Canva, Dubsado, QuickBooks, or HoneyBook. Frustrated by tools that require setup calls.

**Pain points (specific, not generic):**
1. She's using three separate tools (Typeform + Stripe + Notion/spreadsheet) for client intake — and manually copying data between them after every form submission.
2. Her intake forms look nothing like her brand. She spent 45 minutes trying to add her logo to Typeform and gave up.
3. She loses leads because her payment link is a separate step from her intake form — clients fill out the form, then never complete the payment.
4. Every time she onboards a new client, it's a new version of the same 45-minute setup scramble.
5. She's afraid of "powerful" software — the demos always show someone with 10 employees and a dev team.

**What she wants:**
- Something that works without a tutorial
- Forms that look like her brand automatically
- One link that handles intake + payment together
- To spend less than 10 minutes setting up a new client

**Trigger moment:** She just lost a lead because her intake → payment flow had too many steps. She's ready to switch.

---

## Core Message

FormFocus replaces the three-tool intake scramble with one branded form that collects info, takes payment, and syncs to your CRM — in under 10 minutes.

*(One sentence. Do not expand this into a list during scripting. This is the single idea the viewer should leave with.)*

## Hook Strategies

Pick one hook per video. Do not combine. Each hook is a standalone angle — not a variation of the same script.

**Hook 1 — The Frustration Confession (best for TikTok)**
> "I was copying client info from my intake form into a spreadsheet BY HAND. Every. Single. Time."

*Why it works:* Specific, embarrassing, relatable. Every small business owner has done this. The shame of the behavior is the hook — not the product.

**Hook 2 — The Before/After Reveal (best for Instagram, YouTube Shorts)**
> "My old client intake process had 4 steps and a manual payment link. My new one has one link and handles everything."

*Why it works:* Transformation promise in the first 3 seconds. Works well with a screen recording reveal.

**Hook 3 — The Specificity Hook (all platforms)**
> "If you're using Typeform + Stripe + a spreadsheet for client intake, there's a better way."

*Why it works:* Calls out the exact toolstack by name. Viewers recognize themselves immediately. High intent signal.

**Hook 4 — The Time Challenge (TikTok, YouTube Shorts)**
> "I set up a fully branded client intake form with payment collection in 8 minutes. Let me show you."

*Why it works:* Concrete time claim creates a "prove it" reaction. Drives watch-through. Sets up a satisfying demo arc.

## Script Framework

**Structure:** Hook → Problem → Solution → Proof → CTA
**Target duration:** 25–35 seconds (TikTok/Instagram), 35–45 seconds (YouTube Shorts)

### Hook (0–3 seconds)
Open with your chosen hook. Spoken, not text-only. Camera on face or screen. No intro, no "hey guys."

### Problem (3–10 seconds)
Name the specific pain. Be visceral. "Every time I got a new client, I'd spend 20 minutes copying their info from Typeform into my CRM, then send a separate Stripe link, then pray they actually paid." That's the problem. Don't generalize — stay in the specific.

### Solution (10–20 seconds)
Introduce FormFocus as the fix — not as a product with features, but as the thing that makes the problem disappear. Show the product if possible (screen recording, phone, laptop). One demo moment is better than three. Focus on: the branded form going live, the payment + intake in one link, or the CRM sync happening automatically.

### Proof (20–28 seconds)
One concrete proof point. Options:
- "I set up my first form in 8 minutes"
- "I've saved about 3 hours a week on client onboarding since switching"
- "My clients actually complete the form now because the payment is right there"

Do not stack multiple proof points. One is enough. Specificity beats volume.

### CTA (28–35 seconds)
Simple, low-friction ask. "Try it free — link in bio" is sufficient. Do not add urgency language ("limited time," "act now") unless it's a real offer. Do not stack CTAs.

## Tone & Style Guidelines

**The tone:** Direct, unpretentious, and like you figured something out and can't stop telling people about it. This is NOT a polished corporate testimonial. It's a creator who found the fix for a problem they were embarrassed they had.

**Do say:**
- "It just works" — simple, earned credibility
- "I set it up in [specific time]" — concrete beats vague every time
- "My clients actually [do the thing]" — outcome language, not feature language
- Use the word "intake" — it's how this audience already thinks about this workflow

**Don't say:**
- "Revolutionary" or "game-changing" — trust-killers, especially for small business owners who've been burned before
- "Powerful" — this audience is afraid of powerful tools; it signals complexity
- "Seamless" — overused, meaningless
- "All-in-one platform" — feels like enterprise software, not a tool for them

**Visual style:**
- Natural light, real workspace (home office, studio, kitchen table) — not a staged set
- Phone or laptop in frame when doing a demo — show the actual product, not a mockup
- Creator on camera for hook + proof; screen for solution demo
- B-roll: form on screen, CRM sync notification, payment confirmation screen

**What this is NOT:**
- A tutorial (leave deep feature education for YouTube Long)
- A testimonial video with a graphic lower-third
- A voiceover ad with B-roll cutaways
- Anything that looks like it has a media budget

## Platform Notes

### TikTok
- Hook delivery: spoken, 1–3 words of impact, then context. "Hand. Every single time." lands differently than a full sentence opener.
- Duration: aim for 25–30 seconds. Under 30 gets full-view credit.
- Sound ON assumption — score the edit for audio. No silence.
- CTA: "Link in bio" + one comment prompt ("What's your current intake setup?")
- Permission to creators: Use your own words for the problem section. Brief provides the angle; you provide the voice.
- Do NOT make it look like an ad. The moment it looks polished, it loses. Authenticity > production.

### Instagram Reels
- Cover frame matters — design the first frame as a thumbnail. Text overlay with the hook statement works well.
- Sound OFF assumption — every key message needs on-screen text or captions. Do not let the spoken audio carry meaning that isn't also visible.
- Duration: 20–30 seconds. Instagram favors tight.
- Aesthetic: warmer, more considered than TikTok. Creator can be slightly more composed. Still real — not editorial.
- Caption: write the caption as part of the brief deliverable. Suggested caption: "My old client intake had 4 steps and two apps. Now it's one link. [formfocus.io — link in bio]" + relevant hashtags.
- CTA: "Link in bio" + "Save this if you're still doing intake manually"

### YouTube Shorts
- Hook must work as a title statement — it will appear as a static thumbnail in search. "I replaced my 4-step intake process with one link" works as a thumbnail; "I was copying info by hand" is weaker on thumbnail.
- Information density: can go 10–15% deeper on the demo than TikTok. The audience here is slightly more patient and more research-oriented.
- Include a thumbnail concept note: creator face (surprised/relieved expression) + text overlay of the hook statement.
- Subscribe CTA works here; add it after the main CTA: "Follow for more tools I actually use"
- SEO keyword targets: "form builder for small business," "client intake form," "typeform alternative," "form with payment collection," "CRM form sync"
- Duration: 35–45 seconds is ideal for this audience.

## Do's and Don'ts

### Approved Claims
- "FormFocus generates a branded form in under 10 minutes"
- "Collects payment and intake information in a single link"
- "Automatically syncs form responses to your CRM"
- "No code required"
- "Free to start"
- Specific personal time savings from creator's own experience (e.g., "I save about 3 hours a week")

### Off-Limits Claims
- Do NOT claim specific revenue increases or ROI percentages unless the creator can personally verify them
- Do NOT claim "best form builder" or comparative superlatives
- Do NOT reference specific competitor integrations (e.g., "replaces Typeform" is okay; "better than Typeform" is not)
- Do NOT imply FormFocus works with every CRM — approved integrations: HubSpot, Salesforce, Notion, Airtable, GoHighLevel
- Do NOT use the word "free" without qualifying: it's a free trial, not a free product
- Do NOT make enterprise claims (e.g., "scales to any team size") — this product is for small business; stay in that lane

## Success Metrics

| Metric | Target | Notes |
|--------|--------|-------|
| Free trial starts | 2,000 in 30 days | Primary conversion event |
| Cost per trial start | Under $12 | Blended across platforms |
| Hook hold rate (3-sec view %) | 60%+ | Below 50% = hook isn't landing |
| Watch-through rate | 40%+ | Below 35% = problem section is losing them |
| CTR on link in bio | 3%+ | TikTok/Instagram benchmark |
| Save rate (Instagram) | 2%+ | Strong signal for intent |

**Creative health signal:** If a video gets strong watch-through but low CTR, the hook and problem are landing but the CTA or offer isn't compelling. Iterate on the CTA framing before pulling the creative.

**Kill threshold:** Any creative under 40% hook hold rate at $500 spend should be paused and reviewed before scaling.
