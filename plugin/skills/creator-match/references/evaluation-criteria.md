# Creator Evaluation Criteria

## Engagement Rate Benchmarks by Platform

Engagement rate is calculated as (likes + comments + saves + shares) / views or followers, depending on the platform's native reporting. Use the following benchmarks when scoring Platform Performance:

**TikTok:**
- Under 1%: Below average — flag for investigation (may indicate bought followers or declining account health)
- 1-3%: Average — acceptable for established accounts with large followings
- 3-6%: Good — solid signal of an engaged, responsive audience
- 6%+: Strong — prioritize these creators; their audience is actively invested

**Instagram Reels:**
- Under 0.8%: Below average
- 0.8-1.5%: Average
- 1.5-3%: Good
- 3%+: Strong — rare on Instagram; a meaningful signal of high audience trust

**YouTube Shorts:**
- Under 1%: Below average
- 1-2%: Average
- 2-4%: Good
- 4%+: Strong

**LinkedIn (video):**
- Under 1%: Below average
- 1-2%: Average
- 2-5%: Good — LinkedIn audiences are smaller but more professionally engaged
- 5%+: Strong — typically indicates thought leadership traction

Always cross-reference engagement rate against follower count. A 100K creator with 4% engagement outperforms a 1M creator with 0.5% engagement for UGC ad purposes — the smaller audience is more trusting and responsive.

## Red Flags in Creator Profiles

**Fake engagement patterns:**
- Comment-to-like ratio is off (many likes, near-zero comments, or vice versa)
- Comments are generic and non-specific ("great post!", "love this", emoji-only at high volume)
- Follower growth spiked suddenly with no corresponding viral content
- Engagement rate is inconsistent across posts — some posts have 10x the engagement of adjacent posts with no obvious reason

**Competitor conflicts:**
- Creator has worked with a direct competitor in the past 90 days
- Creator's profile or bio mentions a competitor brand
- Creator's audience overlaps heavily with a competitor's own creator roster

**Inconsistent posting:**
- Long gaps (4+ weeks) in posting history with no explanation
- Posting frequency dropped sharply after a previous high-volume period (often signals burnout or pivot away from content creation)
- Recent content is significantly lower quality than older content

**Professionalism signals:**
- Past brands have publicly complained about deliverables or communication
- Creator has a history of posting content and then deleting it at high rates
- Creator's DMs or email response history (if available from past campaigns) shows delays over 72 hours

## The 10-Post Audit Method

Before scoring any creator, review their last 10 posts in order. This audit answers four questions:

1. **Consistency:** Does the creator show up with the same energy, aesthetic, and content quality across posts, or is it highly variable? Consistent creators are lower-risk for brand campaigns.

2. **Style stability:** Has the creator's hook style, pacing, or format shifted dramatically in the last 10 posts? A creator in transition may be experimenting, which is risky for a campaign that needs predictable output.

3. **Audience relationship:** What do the comments look like? Are followers asking questions, sharing personal experiences, or tagging friends? These signals indicate genuine community vs. passive audience.

4. **Brand fit signal:** Imagine the campaign brief dropped into this creator's feed. Does it feel native or forced? If the product would feel jarring against the creator's last 10 posts, it will feel jarring to their audience.

The 10-post audit takes 5-10 minutes and eliminates the most common creator mismatches before they become expensive mistakes.

## Assessing Naturalness vs. Performance in UGC

The most common failure mode in UGC is a creator who reads a script rather than embodies a recommendation. Audiences are highly sensitive to this distinction, even if they can't articulate why.

**Signals of authentic delivery:**
- Creator uses filler language and natural speech patterns (pauses, self-corrections, "like," "honestly")
- Eye contact with camera feels conversational, not rehearsed
- Body language is relaxed — hands move naturally, posture isn't stiff
- Creator deviates slightly from the apparent script with personal observations
- Reactions feel proportionate to the product claim (not over-enthused)

**Signals of performative delivery:**
- Overly smooth delivery with no natural hesitation
- Smile is held too long or applied too uniformly
- Energy is calibrated to "excited" regardless of the product category
- Language is brand-speak ("this revolutionary formula") rather than how a real person would describe something
- Creator doesn't seem to be listening to themselves — the words come out without personal investment

When evaluating, watch a 60-second clip with the sound off first. Body language and facial expression without audio often reveal whether the creator is present or performing.

## Brand Safety Screening Checklist

Run this checklist before finalizing any creator recommendation:

- [ ] Reviewed last 90 days of content for competitor brand mentions
- [ ] Searched creator handle + competitor brand names for any sponsored content
- [ ] Reviewed creator's pinned or featured posts for brand conflicts
- [ ] Checked creator's bio, link-in-bio, and story highlights for active brand partnerships
- [ ] Reviewed creator's comment sections for any brand-negative statements by the creator
- [ ] Searched for any public controversy, callout posts, or brand safety incidents in the past 12 months
- [ ] Verified the creator's content does not contradict the brand's values (e.g., a wellness brand should not use a creator who regularly posts content that contradicts health messaging)
- [ ] Confirmed the creator is not publicly affiliated with political, religious, or social causes that conflict with the brand's positioning or audience
- [ ] If the product is age-gated (alcohol, supplements, financial products): confirmed creator's audience skews to the appropriate age range per platform analytics
