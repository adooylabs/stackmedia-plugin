# Client Intake Question Bank

A complete reference for strategists conducting new client discovery calls. Each section includes primary questions and follow-up probes for vague or incomplete answers.

---

## Campaign

1. **What is the primary goal of this campaign?**
   - Probe if vague: "When you say 'grow the brand,' what does success look like in numbers — followers, purchases, leads?"
2. **What is your single most important KPI for this campaign?**
   - Probe if vague: "If you had to pick one metric to judge whether this campaign worked, what would it be?"
3. **What platforms do you want to run on, and in what priority order?**
   - Probe: "Have you run paid ads on these platforms before, or is this the first time?"
4. **What is your budget range for this campaign, including creator fees and ad spend?**
   - Probe if hesitant: "Even a ballpark helps us scope the right number of creators and variations — are we talking under $10K, $10K-$50K, or over $50K?"
5. **What is your timeline — when do assets need to be ready, and when does the campaign go live?**
   - Probe: "Is there a product launch, sale, or seasonal event driving that date?"
6. **Have you run UGC campaigns before? What worked and what didn't?**
   - Probe: "Do you have performance data from past creative we could learn from?"
7. **Who is the internal decision-maker for creative approvals?**
   - Probe: "How many rounds of revision are typical in your approval process?"

## Brand

1. **How would you describe your brand's tone in three words?**
   - Probe if generic ("professional, friendly, innovative"): "Can you point to a brand outside your category that has a tone you admire? What specifically do you like about it?"
2. **What makes your product meaningfully different from competitors?**
   - Probe: "If a customer who tried your top competitor switched to you, what would they say changed for them?"
3. **What are the top 2-3 features or benefits you want creators to highlight?**
   - Probe: "Of those, which one drives the most conversions when it's in an ad?"
4. **Are there any claims, words, or topics that are strictly off-limits?**
   - Probe: "Any legal, regulatory, or brand guidelines we need to know about before briefing creators?"
5. **Do you have a brand guide, asset library, or existing ad creative we should review?**
   - Probe: "Even informal guidelines — colors you hate, fonts you love — are useful."
6. **What do customers say in reviews that surprises or delights you?**
   - Probe: "Have you seen any organic social posts or testimonials that you wish more people would see?"

## Audience

1. **Who is your primary customer — describe them like a person, not a demographic.**
   - Probe if generic ("women 25-45"): "What does she care about that has nothing to do with your product? What's on her mind on a Tuesday?"
2. **What problem were they trying to solve when they found you?**
   - Probe: "Was it a practical problem (I need X) or an emotional one (I feel Y and I want to feel Z)?"
3. **What are the top 3 reasons someone who should buy your product doesn't?**
   - Probe: "What objection do you hear most often from people who almost bought but didn't?"
4. **What are they using instead of your product right now — direct competitors or workarounds?**
   - Probe: "Do they know those alternatives are worse, or do they think the alternatives are good enough?"
5. **Where does your audience spend time online — which platforms, communities, creators do they follow?**
   - Probe: "Is there a creator or account whose audience closely overlaps with your ideal customer?"

## Creative

1. **Show me an ad from any brand — yours or someone else's — that you think is really good. What do you like about it?**
   - Probe: "What specifically works — the hook, the pacing, the creator's energy, the offer?"
2. **Show me an ad that you hate or would never want associated with your brand. What's wrong with it?**
   - Probe: "Is it the format, the creator type, the messaging, or the production quality?"
3. **What proof points do you have — stats, reviews, clinical data, press mentions, certifications?**
   - Probe: "Do you have any customer quotes or video testimonials we could reference or repurpose?"
4. **Who are your top 2-3 direct competitors, and what are they doing in their ads right now?**
   - Probe: "Is there a competitor you respect creatively, even if you compete with them?"
5. **Are there any creative formats or trends you've seen that you want to try?**
   - Probe: "Have you seen specific TikTok or Reels content that felt right for your brand?"

---

## Creator

1. **Describe your ideal creator — not just demographics, but vibe, lifestyle, energy.**
   - Probe: "Is there a creator you follow who would be perfect for this, or one you've worked with before that you'd want again?"
2. **Are there any creator categories, niches, or aesthetics you want to avoid?**
   - Probe: "Any past creator experiences that went badly — what happened?"
3. **Do you have follower count or platform requirements, or are you open to nano/micro creators?**
   - Probe: "Have you had better results with larger creators or smaller ones in past campaigns?"
4. **Do you need category exclusivity — a creator who isn't working with competitors?**
   - Probe: "How long of an exclusivity window do you need, and for which competitor categories?"
5. **Do you have location, age, or appearance requirements for the creator?**
   - Probe: "Is this a legal requirement (e.g., age-gated product) or a preference?"
6. **Will you be providing product to creators, or do they need to purchase it?**
   - Probe: "Do you have a seeding process, or will StackdMedia handle product fulfillment?"
