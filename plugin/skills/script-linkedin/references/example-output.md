## Variation 2: Thought Leadership (2-3 min)

**Hook type:** Contrarian Take — "Manual admin work is a choice, not a constraint"

---

**Script**

```
[HOOK - 0:00-0:10]
Visual: Creator at desk, direct to camera.
Spoken: "There's a belief in small businesses that manual admin work is just part of the deal. That if you're not big enough to have a dedicated ops team, you eat the friction. I think that's wrong — and it's quietly costing most small business owners more than they realize."
Caption overlay: "Manual admin isn't a constraint. It's a choice."

[CONTEXT - 0:10-0:40]
Visual: Continuous talking head. Creator settles back slightly — establishing mode, not pitching mode.
Spoken: "I've been running operations for small service businesses for about six years. We've gone through the full range — spreadsheets, Zapier stacks, three different CRMs — always trying to patch the holes. The one thing I kept coming back to was forms. Intake forms, request forms, feedback forms. They're the entry point for almost every workflow, and most small teams are handling them completely manually."
Caption overlays: None needed here — let the credibility land naturally.

[CORE ARGUMENT - 0:40-1:50]
Visual: Talking head. Optional: light B-roll of a screen showing a chaotic email inbox or a spreadsheet — conveys "before" without over-producing.
Spoken: "Here's what I mean. A client submits a contact form. It lands in a shared inbox. Someone — usually the same someone — sorts through it, figures out who it belongs to, forwards it to the right person, and then follows up to make sure it didn't fall through the cracks. That's three to four manual touches on a single submission.

If you're getting 20 to 30 form submissions a week, that's somewhere between one and two hours of overhead that nobody is tracking because it's spread across five different people in five-minute increments.

The math is bad. The real problem is that this kind of admin work competes directly with the billable or revenue-generating work that actually grows the business. And because it's invisible overhead — it doesn't show up on a P&L — most operators don't treat it as a priority to fix."
Caption overlays: "3-4 manual touches per submission" → "20-30 forms/week = 1-2 hours of hidden overhead" → "Invisible overhead doesn't show up on a P&L"

[PRODUCT/SOLUTION TIE-IN - 1:50-2:45]
Visual: Brief screen recording of FormFocus — form builder UI, routing logic setup, automated notification to correct team member. Keep to 30-40 seconds max. Return to talking head for close.
Spoken: "Which is exactly why I started using FormFocus about six months ago. It's an AI form builder that handles the routing automatically. You build the form, define the logic — if this type of request comes in, it goes to this person — and it runs without anyone touching it.

The setup for our most complex intake form took about 20 minutes. We haven't manually sorted a submission since.

I'm not saying this solves every ops problem. But if you've got form-based workflows and you're still handling them by hand, this is the most straightforward fix I've found."
Caption overlays: "Set routing logic once" → "20 minutes to set up" → "No manual sorting since"

[CTA - 2:45-3:00]
Visual: Back to direct camera, relaxed close.
Spoken: "There's a free trial — link in the first comment. And if you're running into different kinds of manual overhead in your ops, drop it in the comments. Genuinely curious what's taking up the most time for other small teams."
Caption: "Free trial link in first comment — and drop your biggest ops time sink below"
```

**Format Label:** Thought Leadership (2-5 min)

**Targeting Note:** Target small business owners, founders, and Operations/General Managers at companies with 2-50 employees. Industries: professional services, marketing agencies, consulting, real estate. Job seniority: Owner, Director, Manager. LinkedIn's Thought Leader Ads format is ideal here — sponsor the organic post directly for broader reach while preserving the authentic feel.

**Director's Note:** This needs to feel like a genuine practitioner's perspective, not a brand video. Single-take or minimal cuts — polish the audio, not the edit. Creator should be seated, direct to camera, in a real workspace. No stock footage. The screen recording for the product section should be an actual FormFocus account, not a staged mock-up. Wardrobe: business casual. Energy: calm, analytical, slightly wry — someone who has figured something out and is sharing it plainly, not enthusiastically selling it. The contrarian framing in the hook depends entirely on the creator's delivery feeling measured, not combative.

**Caption (first 150 characters):** Manual admin work in small businesses is treated like a cost of doing business. I think that's wrong — and it's costing most operators more than they track.
